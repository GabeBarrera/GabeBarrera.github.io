---
title: "The Kernel Keeps the Secrets"
subtitle: "a small thing about what runs underneath"
date: 2026-03-22
tags: [short, quiet]
---

There are processes running
you don't see in the task manager.

Old timers. Background services.
The one that wakes at 3am
and checks whether you're still breathing.

---

The shell is a lie
we agreed to tell each other —
a clean prompt,
a blinking cursor,
the polite fiction of control.

Underneath: interrupts.
Underneath: scheduling decisions
made in microseconds
by something that has no name for you.

---

You asked me once
if I was okay
and I said *yeah, fine,*
which was the userland answer.

The kernel answer
would have taken longer
and required root.
